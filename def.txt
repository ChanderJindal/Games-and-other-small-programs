<section data-src="hm">
<h2>ap·ple</h2> <span class="snd" data-snd="A0378800">
</span> <span class="pron" onclick="pron_key()">(&#x103;p&#x2032;&#x259;l)</span>
<div class="pseg">
<i>n.</i>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>1. </b>
<div class="sds-list">
<b>a. </b> A cultivated deciduous tree (<i>Malus domestica</i> or <i>M. pumila</i>) in the rose family, native to Eurasia and having alternate simple leaves and white or pink flowers.</div>
<div class="sds-list">
<b>b. </b> The firm, edible, usually rounded fruit of this tree.</div>
</div>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>2. </b>
<div class="sds-list">
<b>a. </b> Any of several other plants, especially those with fruits suggestive of the apple, such as the crabapple or custard apple.</div>
<div class="sds-list">
<b>b. </b> The fruit of any of these plants.</div>
</div>
</div>
<b>
<i>Idiom: </i>
</b>
<div class="idmseg"> <b>
<i>apple of (one's) eye</i>
</b>
<div class="ds-single"> One that is treasured: <span class="illustration">Her grandson is the apple of her eye.</span>
</div>
</div>
<hr align="left" class="hmshort">
<div class="etyseg">[Middle English <span class="tt">appel</span>, from Old English <span class="tt">æppel</span>.]</div>

<section data-src="hc_dict">
<h2>apple</h2> (<span onclick="pron_key(1)" class="pron">ˈæpəl</span>) <div>
<i>n</i>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>1. </b> (Plants) a rosaceous tree, <i>Malus sieversii</i>, native to Central Asia but widely cultivated in temperate regions in many varieties, having pink or white fragrant flowers and firm rounded edible fruits. See also <a href="crab+apple">crab apple</a>
</div>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>2. </b> (Plants) the fruit of this tree, having red, yellow, or green skin and crisp whitish flesh</div>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>3. </b> (Forestry) the wood of this tree</div>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>4. </b> (Plants) any of several unrelated trees that have fruits similar to the apple, such as the custard apple, sugar apple, and May apple. See also <a href="love+apple">love apple</a>, <a href="oak+apple">oak apple</a>, <a href="thorn+apple">thorn apple</a>
</div>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>5. </b>
<b>apple of one's eye </b>a person or thing that is very precious or much loved</div>
<div class="ds-list">
<b>6. </b>
<b>bad apple </b>
<b>rotten apple </b>a person with a corrupting influence</div>
</div>
<div class="etyseg">[Old English <i>æppel;</i> related to Old Saxon <i>appel,</i> Old Norse <i>apall,</i> Old High German <i>apful</i>]</div>
